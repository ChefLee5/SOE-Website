Scene 3: The Doctor's Office

Felix visits Dr. Esperanza in Vitalis for his yearly checkup. Everything in the office helps keep people healthy.

🖼️ Illustration: The Doctor's Office
A richly detailed scene depicting 'The Doctor's Office' with numbered callout markers (1–40) indicating each vocabulary item's location in the scene.
# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Doctor /DOK-ter/ The doctor asks Felix how he feels today. D-hand or M-hand taps opposite wrist
2 Nurse /NURS/ The nurse takes Felix's temperature first. N-hand taps opposite wrist
3 Patient /PAY-shunt/ Felix is the patient visiting the doctor today. Fingerspell or sign each word
4 Waiting room /WAY-ting ROOM/ Felix reads a magazine in the waiting room. Fingerspell or sign each word
5 Appointment /uh-POYNT-ment/ The appointment is at ten o'clock in the morning. Fingerspell or sign each word
6 Examination room /ig-zam-ih-NAY-shun ROOM/ The examination room has a high table covered in paper. Fingerspell or sign each word
7 Stethoscope /STETH-uh-skohp/ The doctor listens to Felix's heart with a stethoscope. Fingerspell or sign each word
8 Thermometer /ther-MOM-ih-ter/ The thermometer shows that Felix has no fever. Fingerspell or sign each word
9 Blood pressure cuff /BLUD PRESH-er KUF/ The blood pressure cuff wraps around Felix's arm. Fingerspell or sign each word
10 Scale /SKAYL/ Felix stands on the scale to find out his weight. Both flat hands balance like scale
11 Height chart /HYT CHART/ The height chart on the wall shows Felix has grown two inches. Fingerspell or sign each word
12 Syringe /suh-RINJ/ The nurse prepares the syringe for the vaccination. Fingerspell or sign each word
13 Bandage /BAN-dij/ A small bandage covers the spot where Felix got his shot. Mime wrapping bandage around hand
14 Cotton ball /KOT-un BAWL/ The nurse dabs the skin with a soft cotton ball. Fingerspell or sign each word
15 Latex gloves /LAY-teks GLUVZ/ The doctor wears latex gloves during the examination. Fingerspell or sign each word
16 Tongue depressor /TUNG dih-PRES-er/ The tongue depressor helps the doctor look at the throat. Fingerspell or sign each word
17 Eye chart /EYE CHART/ Felix reads the letters on the eye chart across the room. Fingerspell or sign each word
18 Reflex hammer /REE-fleks HAM-er/ The reflex hammer taps gently on the knee. Fingerspell or sign each word
19 Otoscope /OH-tuh-skohp/ The otoscope has a light to look inside the ear. Fingerspell or sign each word
20 X-ray /EKS-ray/ The X-ray shows the bones inside Felix's hand. Fingerspell X-RAY
21 Prescription /prih-SKRIP-shun/ The doctor writes a prescription for medicine. Fingerspell or sign each word
22 Diagnosis /dy-ag-NOH-sis/ The diagnosis is the doctor's explanation of what is wrong. Fingerspell or sign each word
23 Symptom /SIMP-tum/ A cough is a common symptom of a cold. Fingerspell or sign each word
24 Fever /FEE-ver/ A fever means your body temperature is higher than normal. Back of hand on forehead (checking temp)
25 Allergy /AL-er-jee/ Felix has an allergy to pollen that makes him sneeze. Fingerspell or sign each word
26 Vaccine /vak-SEEN/ A vaccine helps the body fight serious diseases. Fingerspell or sign each word
27 Check-up /CHEK-up/ A yearly check-up keeps you healthy. Fingerspell or sign each word
28 Insurance card /in-SHOOR-uns KARD/ Amara shows her insurance card at the front desk. Fingerspell or sign each word
29 Referral /rih-FUR-ul/ The doctor gives a referral to see a specialist. Fingerspell or sign each word
30 Vital signs /VY-tul SYNZ/ Vital signs include heart rate, breathing, and temperature. Fingerspell or sign each word
31 Blood test /BLUD TEST/ A blood test checks for infections and nutrients. Fingerspell or sign each word
32 Cast /KAST/ A plaster cast keeps a broken bone in place while it heals. Fingerspell CAST
33 Crutches /KRUCH-ez/ Crutches help you walk when your leg is injured. Fingerspell or sign each word
34 Wheelchair /WEEL-chair/ A wheelchair provides mobility for people who cannot walk. Fingerspell or sign each word
35 First aid kit /FURST AYD KIT/ The first aid kit has bandages, antiseptic, and tape. Fingerspell or sign each word
36 Emergency /ih-MUR-jun-see/ Call 911 in an emergency for immediate help. Fingerspell or sign each word
37 Ambulance /AM-byoo-luns/ The ambulance rushes to the hospital with its sirens on. Fingerspell or sign each word
38 Hospital /HOS-pih-tul/ The hospital is where people go for serious medical care. H-hand draws cross on upper arm
39 Surgery /SUR-jer-ee/ Surgery is an operation to fix something inside the body. Fingerspell or sign each word
40 Recovery /rih-KUV-er-ee/ Recovery is the time it takes to get better after being sick. Fingerspell or sign each word

Felix's Tip:

"Practice saying these words to someone you trust. If you ever need to visit a doctor, knowing these words will help you describe what you feel. 'I have a symptom. I have a fever.' See? You can do it!"
