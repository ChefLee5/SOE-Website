# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Beat /BEET/ Clap on every beat of the music. Fist taps chest rhythmically
2 Tempo /TEM-poh/ A fast tempo makes you want to dance. Index bounces at varying speeds
3 Measure /MEZH-er/ Each measure has four beats. Both Y-hands tap together (SAME SIZE)
4 Rest /REST/ A rest is a moment of silence in the music. Arms cross over chest (resting)
5 Duration /dur-AY-shun/ The duration of the song is three minutes. D-hand slides along opposite forearm (length of time)
6 Whole note /HOHL NOHT/ A whole note lasts four beats. Fingerspell or sign each word
7 Half note /HAF NOHT/ A half note lasts two beats. Fingerspell or sign each word
8 Quarter note /KWOR-ter NOHT/ A quarter note gets one beat. Fingerspell or sign each word
9 Eighth note /AYTTH NOHT/ An eighth note is half a beat. Fingerspell or sign each word
10 Time signature /TYM SIG-nuh-chur/ The time signature tells how many beats per measure. Fingerspell or sign each word
11 Metronome /MET-roh-nohm/ The metronome clicks to keep steady time. Index swings side to side steadily
12 Rhythm /RITH-um/ Feel the rhythm in your body. R-hand bounces forward rhythmically
13 Syncopation /sin-koh-PAY-shun/ Syncopation puts the beat in unexpected places. Fingerspell or sign each word
14 Upbeat /UP-beet/ The upbeat comes before the downbeat. Fingerspell UPBEAT
15 Downbeat /DOWN-beet/ The downbeat is the first beat of the measure. Fingerspell or sign each word
16 Repeat /rih-PEET/ Repeat the chorus one more time. Fingerspell REPEAT
17 Verse /VURS/ The verse tells the story of the song. Fingerspell VERSE
18 Chorus /KOR-us/ The chorus is the part everyone sings along to. Fingerspell CHORUS
19 Bridge /BRIJ/ The bridge connects the verse to the chorus. V-hand bridges under opposite forearm
20 Finale /fih-NAL-ee/ The finale is the exciting ending of the performance. Both 5-hands shake and drop (FINISH + BIG)
21 Encore /ON-kor/ The crowd shouted encore for one more song. Index circles then points forward (MORE)
22 Crescendo /kreh-SHEN-doh/ A crescendo means the music gets louder. Fingerspell or sign each word
23 Pause /PAWZ/ There was a dramatic pause before the last note. Both flat hands freeze in air
24 Timing /TY-ming/ Good timing means playing the note at the right moment. T-hand taps wrist precisely
25 Countdown /KOWNT-down/ The conductor gives a countdown before the first note. Fingers count down 5-4-3-2-1
