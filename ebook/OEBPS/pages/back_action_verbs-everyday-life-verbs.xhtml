Scene 2: Everyday Life Verbs

From morning to night, these verbs describe everything you do.

🖼️ Illustration: Everyday Life Verbs
A richly detailed scene depicting 'Everyday Life Verbs' with numbered callout markers (1–25) indicating each vocabulary item's location in the scene.
# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Eat /EET/ Eat a healthy breakfast every morning. Flat O taps mouth repeatedly
2 Drink /DRINK/ Drink eight glasses of water a day. C-hand tips to mouth
3 Cook /KOOK/ I cook dinner for my family every night. Flip hand on opposite palm (like flipping pancake)
4 Clean /KLEEN/ Clean your room before you go out. Flat hand slides across opposite palm
5 Wash /WOSH/ Wash your hands before eating. Fists rub together (scrubbing)
6 Open /OH-pen/ Open the window to let fresh air in. B-hands together, swing apart
7 Close /KLOHZ/ Close the door behind you. B-hands apart, swing together
8 Turn on /TURN ON/ Turn on the light, please. Fingerspell or sign each word
9 Turn off /TURN OFF/ Turn off the TV before bed. Fingerspell or sign each word
10 Buy /BY/ Buy milk at the grocery store. Flat hand on opposite palm, lift away
11 Pay /PAY/ Pay for the groceries at the register. Index flicks off opposite palm
12 Give /GIV/ Give the book to your friend. Both flat O-hands push forward opening
13 Take /TAYK/ Take your medicine twice a day. 5-hand grabs from air, pulls to body as S-hand
14 Put /PUT/ Put the dishes in the sink. Flat O-hands drop forward, open
15 Get /GET/ Get ready for school by seven thirty. Fingerspell GET
16 Make /MAYK/ Make your bed every morning. S-fists stack, twist and stack again
17 Read /REED/ Read a book to your child before bed. V-fingers move down opposite flat palm
18 Write /RYT/ Write your name at the top of the page. Pinch fingers, write on opposite palm
19 Listen /LIS-en/ Listen carefully to the instructions. Cup hand behind ear
20 Speak /SPEEK/ Speak slowly so I can understand. 4-fingers tap near chin repeatedly
21 Ask /ASK/ Ask the teacher if you have a question. Fingerspell ASK
22 Answer /AN-ser/ Answer the phone when it rings. Index from chin points forward
23 Help /HELP/ Can you help me carry this box? Fist on flat palm, lift both up
24 Try /TRY/ Try your best on every test. T-hands push forward with effort
25 Learn /LURN/ I want to learn English fluently. Flat hand lifts from opposite palm to forehead
