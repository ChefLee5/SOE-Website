# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Sundial /SUN-dy-ul/ The sundial uses shadows to tell time. S-hand + index traces shadow on palm
2 Hourglass /OW-er-glas/ Flip the hourglass to start the timer. Both C-hands stack, flip (turning hourglass)
3 Pendulum /PEN-joo-lum/ The pendulum swings back and forth in the clock. Index swings back and forth below fist
4 Candle clock /KAN-dul KLOK/ A candle clock burns down to mark the hours. Fingerspell or sign each word
5 Water clock /WOT-er KLOK/ The water clock drips to measure time passing. Fingerspell or sign each word
6 Pocket watch /POK-it WOTCH/ Grandfather keeps a pocket watch on a chain. Fingerspell or sign each word
7 Wristwatch /RIST-wotch/ Check the wristwatch to see if we are late. Fingerspell or sign each word
8 Grandfather clock /GRAND-fah-ther KLOK/ The grandfather clock chimes every hour. Fingerspell or sign each word
9 Cuckoo clock /KOO-koo KLOK/ The cuckoo clock has a little bird that pops out. Fingerspell or sign each word
10 Clocktower /KLOK-tow-er/ Big Ben is a famous clocktower. Fingerspell or sign each word
11 Timekeeper /TYM-kee-per/ The timekeeper records the runners' times. Fingerspell or sign each word
12 Chronometer /kroh-NOM-eh-ter/ A chronometer is an extremely accurate clock. Fingerspell or sign each word
13 Dial /DY-ul/ The dial shows the numbers one through twelve. Fingerspell DIAL
14 Hands /HANDZ/ The clock hands point to the hour and minute. Flat hand chops opposite wrist (repeated)
15 Tick /TIK/ The clock goes tick tock every second. Fingerspell TICK
16 Chime /CHYM/ The clock chimes at noon and midnight. Fingerspell CHIME
17 Ancient /AYN-shent/ Ancient civilizations measured time with the stars. S-hand pulls wisdom from chin repeatedly
18 Modern /MOD-ern/ Modern clocks use quartz crystals. M-hand flicks forward from chin (NEW)
19 Invention /in-VEN-shun/ The clock was one of history's greatest inventions. 4-hand pulls from forehead (IDEA + MAKE)
20 Accurate /AK-yur-it/ Atomic clocks are the most accurate. Both F-hands touch precisely
21 Timezone /TYM-zohn/ The world has twenty-four timezones. T + Z, then point across horizon
22 Greenwich /GREN-itch/ Greenwich Mean Time is the world standard. Fingerspell or sign each word
23 AM /AY-EM/ AM means before noon. Fingerspell AM
24 PM /PEE-EM/ PM means after noon. Fingerspell PM
25 Era /EER-uh/ Each era of history has its own timekeeping method. E-hand circles in time motion
