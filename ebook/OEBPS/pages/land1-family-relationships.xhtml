# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Family /FAM-uh-lee/ Kenji's family sits together at the long table. F-hands circle outward from center
2 Mother /MUTH-er/ His mother makes rice and fish every Sunday. Open 5-hand, thumb taps chin
3 Father /FAH-ther/ His father teaches music in the village school. Open 5-hand, thumb taps forehead
4 Parent /PAIR-unt/ A parent takes care of their children. Open 5 at forehead, then chin
5 Son /SUN/ Kenji is the eldest son in his family. Sign BOY + BABY (rock arms)
6 Daughter /DAW-ter/ Aiko is the youngest daughter. Sign GIRL + BABY (rock arms)
7 Brother /BRUTH-er/ Kenji has one older brother who plays drums. A-hand forehead to L-hand (BOY + SAME)
8 Sister /SIS-ter/ Aiko's sister teaches her new songs. A-hand chin to L-hand (GIRL + SAME)
9 Grandmother /GRAND-muth-er/ Grandmother tells stories by the fire. Open 5 at chin, arc forward twice
10 Grandfather /GRAND-fah-ther/ Grandfather built their house with his own hands. Open 5 at forehead, arc forward twice
11 Grandparent /GRAND-pair-unt/ A grandparent is the parent of your parent. Fingerspell or sign each word
12 Aunt /ANT/ Aiko's aunt brings fresh flowers every week. A-hand shakes near cheek
13 Uncle /UNG-kul/ Kenji's uncle is a fisherman. U-hand shakes near forehead
14 Cousin /KUZ-in/ My cousin and I are the same age. C-hand shakes near ear
15 Niece /NEES/ My niece is my sister's daughter. Fingerspell NIECE
16 Nephew /NEF-yoo/ My nephew is my brother's son. Fingerspell NEPHEW
17 Husband /HUZ-bund/ Kenji's father is his mother's husband. Clasp hands (MARRY) + BOY sign
18 Wife /WYF/ Kenji's mother is his father's wife. Clasp hands (MARRY) + GIRL sign
19 Baby /BAY-bee/ The baby sleeps in a small crib. Rock arms like cradling baby
20 Toddler /TOD-ler/ The toddler is learning to walk. Fingerspell or sign each word
21 Child /CHYLD/ Every child in Harmonia learns music first. Pat downward in air (showing height)
22 Teenager /TEEN-ay-jer/ The teenager helps younger children with homework. Fingerspell or sign each word
23 Adult /uh-DULT/ An adult has more responsibilities than a child. A-hand moves from forehead to chest
24 Elder /EL-der/ The village elder shares wisdom from long ago. Fingerspell ELDER
25 Stepmother /STEP-muth-er/ A stepmother joins the family through marriage. Fingerspell or sign each word
26 Stepfather /STEP-fah-ther/ A stepfather loves his stepchildren as his own. Fingerspell or sign each word
27 Half-brother /HAF-bruth-er/ A half-brother shares one parent with you. Fingerspell or sign each word
28 Half-sister /HAF-sis-ter/ A half-sister shares one parent with you. Fingerspell or sign each word
29 Foster child /FOS-ter CHYLD/ A foster child lives with a caring family temporarily. Fingerspell or sign each word
30 Adopted /uh-DOP-ted/ An adopted child is chosen and loved into a family. Fingerspell or sign each word
31 Generation /jen-er-AY-shun/ Three generations live together in Harmonia. Fingerspell or sign each word
32 Twin /TWIN/ The twins look exactly alike! Fingerspell TWIN
33 Sibling /SIB-ling/ A sibling is a brother or sister. Fingerspell or sign each word
34 Relative /REL-uh-tiv/ A relative is someone in your extended family. Fingerspell or sign each word
35 Gather /GATH-er/ The family gathers together for holidays. Fingerspell GATHER

Kenji's Tip:

"Draw a family tree! Put your grandparents at the top, your parents in the middle, and you at the bottom. Write each person's family word: MOTHER, FATHER, GRANDMOTHER. That's YOUR family tree in English!"
