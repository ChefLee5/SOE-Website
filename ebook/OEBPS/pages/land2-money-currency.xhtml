# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Money /MUN-ee/ Money is used to buy things you need. Flat O taps opposite palm twice
2 Dollar /DOL-er/ One dollar equals one hundred cents. Flat hand grasps and slides off opposite hand
3 Cent /SENT/ A cent is the smallest unit of money. Fingerspell CENT
4 Penny /PEN-ee/ A penny is worth one cent and is copper-colored. Fingerspell PENNY
5 Nickel /NIK-ul/ A nickel is worth five cents. Fingerspell NICKEL
6 Dime /DYM/ A dime is worth ten cents and is the smallest coin. Fingerspell DIME
7 Quarter /KWOR-ter/ A quarter is worth twenty-five cents. Fingerspell or sign each word
8 Bill /BIL/ A one-dollar bill is green and paper. Fingerspell BILL
9 Five-dollar bill /FYV-DOL-er BIL/ Kwame has a five-dollar bill in his wallet. Fingerspell or sign each word
10 Ten-dollar bill /TEN-DOL-er BIL/ The ten-dollar bill has a picture of Alexander Hamilton. Fingerspell or sign each word
11 Twenty-dollar bill /TWEN-tee-DOL-er BIL/ Octavia pays with a twenty-dollar bill. Fingerspell or sign each word
12 Change /CHAYNJ/ The cashier gives back change after you pay. A-fists switch positions (swap)
13 Wallet /WAL-it/ Keep your money safe in your wallet. Fingerspell WALLET
14 Purse /PURS/ Octavia keeps her wallet inside her purse. Fingerspell PURSE
15 Cash /KASH/ Some stores only accept cash, not cards. Fingerspell CASH
16 Credit card /KRED-it KARD/ A credit card lets you buy now and pay later. Fingerspell or sign each word
17 Debit card /DEB-it KARD/ A debit card takes money directly from your bank. Fingerspell or sign each word
18 Check /CHEK/ Write a check to pay the rent. Fingerspell CHECK
19 Coins /KOYNZ/ The coins jingle in Kwame's pocket. Index traces circle on opposite palm (repeated)
20 Price /PRYS/ The price tag shows how much something costs. F-hand strikes opposite palm downward
21 Cost /KOST/ How much does this book cost? X-hand slides down opposite palm
22 Expensive /ek-SPEN-siv/ The bicycle is too expensive at three hundred dollars. Flat O on palm, flick open going up
23 Cheap /CHEEP/ The notebook is cheap at only two dollars. B-hand curves down off opposite palm
24 Affordable /uh-FOR-duh-bul/ Find an affordable phone plan that fits your budget. Fingerspell or sign each word
25 Budget /BUJ-it/ A budget helps you plan how to spend your money. B-hands on opposite palm (MONEY + PLAN)
26 Save /SAYV/ Save a little money each week for emergencies. Fingerspell SAVE
27 Spend /SPEND/ Don't spend more money than you earn. Fingerspell SPEND
28 Earn /URN/ Kwame earns money by helping in the garden. Fingerspell EARN
29 Borrow /BOR-oh/ Borrow money only when you know you can pay it back. Fingerspell BORROW
30 Owe /OH/ If you borrow ten dollars, you owe ten dollars. Fingerspell OWE
31 Tip /TIP/ A tip is extra money you give for good service. Fingerspell TIP
32 Receipt /rih-SEET/ Keep the receipt in case you need to return the item. Fingerspell or sign each word
33 Tax /TAKS/ Sales tax is added to the price of most items. T-hand or X-hand taps opposite palm
34 Discount /DIS-kownt/ A discount lowers the original price. Fingerspell or sign each word
35 Sale /SAYL/ Everything is twenty percent off during the sale. Fingerspell SALE
