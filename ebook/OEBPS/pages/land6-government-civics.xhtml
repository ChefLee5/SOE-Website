# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Government /GUV-ern-ment/ The government makes laws to protect citizens. Fingerspell or sign each word
2 President /PREZ-ih-dent/ The president is the leader of the country. Both C-hands at temples, rise to close as fists
3 Governor /GUV-er-ner/ The governor leads the state government. G-hand circles at temple
4 Mayor /MAY-er/ The mayor leads the city government. M-hand at shoulder, like official
5 Law /LAW/ A law is a rule that everyone must follow. L-hand taps opposite flat palm (LAW or RULE)
6 Right /RYT/ Every person has the right to free speech. R-hand rises (right/entitlement)
7 Vote /VOHT/ Citizens vote to choose their leaders. F-hand drops into opposite O-hand (putting ballot in box)
8 Election /ih-LEK-shun/ An election is when people vote for their leaders. E-hand + VOTE motion
9 Constitution /kon-stih-TOO-shun/ The Constitution is the highest law of the land. Fingerspell or sign each word
10 Amendment /uh-MEND-ment/ An amendment is a change or addition to the Constitution. A-hand rewrites on opposite palm
11 Court /KORT/ Legal disputes are settled in court. C-hands outline rectangle
12 Judge /JUJ/ The judge makes decisions based on the law. Fingerspell JUDGE
13 Jury /JUR-ee/ A jury is a group of citizens who decide a court case. Fingerspell JURY
14 Trial /TRY-ul/ The trial determines if the person is guilty or not guilty. Fingerspell TRIAL
15 Citizen /SIT-ih-zen/ A citizen has both rights and responsibilities. Both C-hands touch shoulders then meet at center
16 Tax /TAKS/ Citizens pay taxes to fund public services. T-hand or X-hand taps opposite palm
17 Community /kuh-MYOO-nih-tee/ A community is a group of people living in the same area. Fingerspell or sign each word
18 Neighborhood /NAY-ber-hood/ Athena knows everyone in her neighborhood. Fingerspell or sign each word
19 Democracy /deh-MOK-ruh-see/ In a democracy, the people choose their leaders. D-hands circle each other
20 Freedom /FREE-dum/ Freedom means the ability to act and speak without restraint. Fingerspell or sign each word
21 Justice /JUS-tis/ Justice means fairness and equal treatment for everyone. Fingerspell or sign each word
22 Liberty /LIB-er-tee/ The Statue of Liberty symbolizes freedom. Fingerspell or sign each word
23 Flag /FLAG/ The flag of a country represents its people and values. Fingerspell FLAG
24 Pledge /PLEJ/ Students recite the Pledge of Allegiance each morning. Fingerspell PLEDGE
25 National anthem /NASH-un-ul AN-them/ Stand respectfully when the national anthem plays. Fingerspell or sign each word
