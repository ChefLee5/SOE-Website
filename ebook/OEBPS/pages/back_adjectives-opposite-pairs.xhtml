🎨 Descriptive Words (Adjectives)

Guided by All Rhythm Quest Heroes

Scene 1: Opposite Pairs

Descriptive words come in pairs! Learning opposites doubles your vocabulary instantly.

🖼️ Illustration: Opposite Pairs
A richly detailed scene depicting 'Opposite Pairs' with numbered callout markers (1–25) indicating each vocabulary item's location in the scene.
# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Big /BIG/ The elephant is big. Both L-hands spread apart (expanding)
2 Small /SMAWL/ The ant is very small. Both flat hands come close together
3 Hot /HOT/ Be careful — the soup is hot. C-hand at mouth, flick away sharply
4 Cold /KOHLD/ Wear a coat when it is cold outside. Both fists shake near body (shivering)
5 Fast /FAST/ The rabbit runs fast. L-hands pull back quickly (snapping)
6 Slow /SLOH/ The turtle moves slow and steady. Hand drags slowly up opposite forearm
7 Happy /HAP-ee/ The children are happy at the party. Flat hand brushes up chest twice
8 Sad /SAD/ She felt sad when her friend moved away. Both open hands slide down face
9 Old /OHLD/ The old tree has been here for a hundred years. S-hand pulls down from chin (beard)
10 New /NOO/ I bought a new pair of shoes. Curved hand scoops across opposite palm
11 Full /FOOL/ The glass is full of water. Flat hand slides across top of opposite fist
12 Empty /EMP-tee/ The refrigerator is empty — time to go shopping. Middle finger draws across back of opposite hand
13 Long /LONG/ The long road stretches for miles. Index draws along opposite arm (length)
14 Short /SHORT/ The short answer is yes. Bent hand pushes down
15 Tall /TAWL/ The tall building has fifty floors. Index draws upward on opposite palm
16 Heavy /HEV-ee/ The suitcase is too heavy to carry. Both flat hands push down slowly
17 Light /LYT/ This bag is light enough for a child. Both flat hands rise up easily
18 Loud /LOWD/ The music is too loud — please turn it down. Point to ear, then both fists shake out
19 Quiet /KWY-et/ The library is a quiet place to study. Index finger on lips, hands cross and separate
20 Hard /HARD/ The math problem was very hard. Bent V hits opposite palm (solid)
21 Easy /EE-zee/ The first question was easy. Curved hand brushes up off opposite fingers
22 Clean /KLEEN/ Keep your workspace clean and organized. Flat hand slides across opposite palm
23 Dirty /DUR-tee/ Wash the dirty dishes after dinner. 5-hand under chin, wiggle fingers
24 Safe /SAYF/ This neighborhood is safe for children. S-hands cross at wrists, then pull apart
25 Dangerous /DAYN-jer-us/ Swimming alone is dangerous. A-fist pushes up past opposite A-fist
