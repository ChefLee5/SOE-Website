# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Address /AD-res/ Write your address so people know where you live. A-hands move up chest (like suspenders)
2 Street /STREET/ Kenji lives on Maple Street in Harmonia. Both S-hands move forward parallel
3 City /SIT-ee/ The city is a large place with many buildings. Triangle rooftops with both hands, twist
4 State /STAYT/ Each state has its own capital city. S-hand taps opposite flat palm
5 Zip code /ZIP kohd/ The zip code helps the mail find your house. Fingerspell or sign each word
6 Country /KUN-tree/ People in Harmonia come from every country. Y-hand circles elbow
7 Phone number /FOHN NUM-ber/ Write your phone number on the line. Sign PHONE + NUMBER
8 Email /EE-mayl/ Send me an email if you have any questions. Index shoots into opposite C-hand (mail + computer)
9 Date of birth /DAYT ov BURTH/ Your date of birth is the day you were born. Fingerspell or sign each word
10 Age /AYJ/ Kenji is ten years of age. Pull chin down with C-hand
11 Male /MAYL/ Check the box that says male or female. Fingerspell MALE
12 Female /FEE-mayl/ The form asks if you are male or female. Fingerspell FEMALE
13 Signature /SIG-nuh-chur/ Write your signature at the bottom of the form. Fingerspell or sign each word
14 Social Security number /SOH-shul sih-KYOOR-ih-tee NUM-ber/ A Social Security number is private information. Fingerspell or sign each word
15 Identification /eye-den-tih-fih-KAY-shun/ Bring your identification to the appointment. Fingerspell or sign each word
16 Driver's license /DRY-verz LY-sens/ A driver's license has your photo and address. Fingerspell or sign each word
17 Passport /PAS-port/ A passport lets you travel to other countries. P-hand opens like small book
18 Apartment number /uh-PART-ment NUM-ber/ The apartment number is 4B on the second floor. Fingerspell or sign each word
19 Marital status /MAIR-ih-tul STAT-us/ Marital status means single, married, or divorced. Fingerspell or sign each word
20 Single /SING-gul/ Single means you are not married. Fingerspell SINGLE
21 Married /MAIR-eed/ Married means you have a husband or wife. Fingerspell or sign each word
22 Emergency contact /ih-MUR-jun-see KON-takt/ Write the name of your emergency contact. Fingerspell or sign each word
23 Form /FORM/ Fill out the form with a dark pen. Fingerspell FORM
24 Application /ap-lih-KAY-shun/ The job application asks about your work experience. Fingerspell or sign each word
25 Print /PRINT/ Please print your name clearly in block letters. Fingerspell PRINT
26 Spell /SPEL/ Can you spell your last name for me? Fingerspell SPELL
27 Citizen /SIT-ih-zen/ A citizen has the right to vote. Both C-hands touch shoulders then meet at center
28 Resident /REZ-ih-dent/ A resident is someone who lives in a place. Fingerspell or sign each word
29 Birth certificate /BURTH ser-TIF-ih-kit/ A birth certificate proves your date and place of birth. Fingerspell or sign each word
30 Legal /LEE-gul/ A legal document is recognized by the government. Fingerspell LEGAL

Aiko's Tip:

"Practice filling out forms! Write your name, address, and phone number. Do it until it feels easy. These words keep you safe and connected."
