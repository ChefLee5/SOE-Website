# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Date /DAYT/ What is today's date? Fingerspell DATE
2 Day /DAY/ There are seven days in a week. Index arm rests on opposite, arcs like sun
3 Week /WEEK/ The project is due next week. Index moves across opposite flat palm (7 days)
4 Month /MUNTH/ January is the first month. Index slides down opposite index once
5 Year /YEER/ A year has three hundred sixty-five days. S-fists circle each other, then stack
6 Decade /DEK-ayd/ A decade is ten years. Fingerspell DECADE
7 Century /SEN-chur-ee/ A century is one hundred years. Fingerspell or sign each word
8 Leap year /LEEP YEER/ A leap year has three sixty-six days. Fingerspell or sign each word
9 Quarter /KWOR-ter/ Each quarter has three months. Fingerspell or sign each word
10 Semester /seh-MES-ter/ Fall semester begins in August. Fingerspell or sign each word
11 Weekend /WEEK-end/ Saturday and Sunday are the weekend. Fingerspell or sign each word
12 Weekday /WEEK-day/ School is open on weekdays only. Fingerspell or sign each word
13 Yesterday /YES-ter-day/ Yesterday was Tuesday. Y-hand or A-thumb touches cheek, then ear
14 Today /too-DAY/ Today is Wednesday. Both Y-hands drop in front of body
15 Tomorrow /too-MOR-oh/ Tomorrow will be Thursday. Thumb of A-hand bounces forward from cheek
16 Morning /MOR-ning/ Class starts at eight in the morning. Arm rises from opposite forearm (sunrise)
17 Afternoon /af-ter-NOON/ The meeting is at two in the afternoon. Forearm tilts forward from opposite forearm
18 Evening /EEV-ning/ We eat dinner in the evening. Hand curves down on opposite forearm (sunset)
19 Midnight /MID-nyt/ The clock struck midnight. Hand drops below opposite forearm (sun below)
20 Noon /NOON/ Lunch is served at noon. Forearm straight up on opposite forearm
21 Dawn /DAWN/ The sun rises at dawn. Fingerspell DAWN
22 Dusk /DUSK/ The sky turns orange at dusk. Fingerspell DUSK
23 Annual /AN-yoo-ul/ The annual parade is on July fourth. Fingerspell ANNUAL
24 Biweekly /by-WEEK-lee/ She gets paid biweekly. Fingerspell or sign each word
25 Schedule /SKEJ-ool/ Check the bus schedule. 4-hand slides down, then across opposite palm
