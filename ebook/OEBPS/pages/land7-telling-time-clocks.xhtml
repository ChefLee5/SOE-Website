# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Clock /KLOK/ The clock on the wall shows it is ten fifteen. Point to wrist + mime clock hands
2 Watch /WOCH/ Selene wears a watch on her wrist to tell time. Point to wrist (watch)
3 Hour /OW-er/ An hour is sixty minutes long. Index circles on opposite flat palm (clock hand)
4 Minute /MIN-it/ Each minute is sixty seconds long. Index finger ticks on opposite palm
5 Second /SEK-und/ A second is the smallest common unit of time. Index finger flicks behind opposite palm
6 Quarter past /KWOR-ter PAST/ It is quarter past three — that means 3:15. Fingerspell or sign each word
7 Half past /HAF PAST/ Half past six means 6:30. Fingerspell or sign each word
8 Quarter to /KWOR-ter TOO/ Quarter to nine means 8:45. Fingerspell or sign each word
9 Noon /NOON/ Noon is twelve o'clock in the daytime. Forearm straight up on opposite forearm
10 A.M. /AY-EM/ A.M. means the morning hours, before noon. Fingerspell A.M.
11 P.M. /PEE-EM/ P.M. means the afternoon and evening hours. Fingerspell P.M.
12 Alarm /uh-LARM/ Set the alarm for seven A.M. to wake up for school. Index taps opposite palm rapidly (ringing)
13 Timer /TY-mer/ Set a timer for twenty minutes to bake the cookies. T-hand taps wrist
14 Stopwatch /STOP-woch/ Use the stopwatch to time the race. Both S-hands: one taps other (START + WATCH)
15 Sundial /SUN-dy-ul/ A sundial uses the sun's shadow to tell time. S-hand + index traces shadow on palm
16 Hourglass /OW-er-glas/ Sand flows through the hourglass in exactly one hour. Both C-hands stack, flip (turning hourglass)
17 Time zone /TYM ZOHN/ New York and Los Angeles are in different time zones. Fingerspell or sign each word
18 On time /ON TYM/ Always try to be on time for appointments. Fingerspell or sign each word
19 Late /LAYT/ If you leave at eight, you will be late for class. Hand waves downward beside hip
20 Early /UR-lee/ Arriving early gives you time to prepare. Middle finger slides over fist knuckle

Elias's Tip:

"Practice telling time all day! Look at a clock and say: 'It is ___.' Try: 'quarter past,' 'half past,' and 'quarter to.' Time is the one English skill you use EVERY SINGLE DAY."
