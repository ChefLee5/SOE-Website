# Word Pronunciation In the Story… ASL Sign 🤟 My Language
1 Clock /KLOK/ The clock on the tower has two hands. Point to wrist + mime clock hands
2 Watch /WOCH/ Kwame wears a watch on his left wrist. Point to wrist (watch)
3 Hour /OW-er/ One hour equals sixty minutes. Index circles on opposite flat palm (clock hand)
4 Minute /MIN-it/ One minute equals sixty seconds. Index finger ticks on opposite palm
5 Second /SEK-und/ A second is the smallest common unit of time. Index finger flicks behind opposite palm
6 Noon /NOON/ Noon is twelve o'clock in the middle of the day. Forearm straight up on opposite forearm
7 Midnight /MID-nyt/ Midnight is twelve o'clock at night. Hand drops below opposite forearm (sun below)
8 A.M. /AY-EM/ A.M. means before noon — morning time. Fingerspell A.M.
9 P.M. /PEE-EM/ P.M. means after noon — afternoon and evening. Fingerspell P.M.
10 O'clock /oh-KLOK/ School starts at eight o'clock. Fingerspell or sign each word
11 Half past /HAF PAST/ It's half past three — time for a snack! Fingerspell or sign each word
12 Quarter past /KWOR-ter PAST/ It's quarter past nine — class started fifteen minutes ago. Fingerspell or sign each word
13 Quarter to /KWOR-ter TOO/ It's quarter to five — almost dinner time. Fingerspell or sign each word
14 Hour hand /OW-er HAND/ The short hour hand points to the hour. Fingerspell or sign each word
15 Minute hand /MIN-it HAND/ The long minute hand counts the minutes. Fingerspell or sign each word
16 Alarm /uh-LARM/ Set the alarm for six thirty in the morning. Index taps opposite palm rapidly (ringing)
17 Timer /TY-mer/ Set the timer for ten minutes. T-hand taps wrist
18 Stopwatch /STOP-woch/ Use the stopwatch to time the race. Both S-hands: one taps other (START + WATCH)
19 Schedule /SKED-yool/ Check the schedule for the bus arrival time. 4-hand slides down, then across opposite palm
20 On time /ON TYM/ Kwame is always on time for class. Fingerspell or sign each word
21 Late /LAYT/ Octavia was late because the bus was delayed. Hand waves downward beside hip
22 Early /UR-lee/ Arriving early gives you time to prepare. Middle finger slides over fist knuckle
23 Duration /doo-RAY-shun/ The duration of the movie is two hours. D-hand slides along opposite forearm (length of time)
24 Sunrise /SUN-ryz/ Sunrise is when the sun comes up in the morning. Fingerspell or sign each word
25 Sunset /SUN-set/ Sunset paints the sky orange and pink. Fingerspell SUNSET

Kwame's Tip:

"Find a clock right now. What time is it? Say it in English: 'It is three fifteen.' Now say it another way: 'It is quarter past three.' Two ways to say the same time!"
