## Scene 1: ASL Alphabet A–M

*Every letter has a handshape. Learn the ASL Manual Alphabet — your bridge to fingerspelling any English word!*

| # | Word | Phonetic | In the Story... | ASL Sign | My Language |
|---|------|----------|----------------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | A | /AY/ | The letter A starts the alphabet. | Fist with thumb at side | ___________ |
| 2 | B | /BEE/ | B is for Book, Bus, and Banana. | Four fingers up, thumb across palm | ___________ |
| 3 | C | /SEE/ | C is for Cat, City, and Cup. | Curved hand like the letter C | ___________ |
| 4 | D | /DEE/ | D is for Dog, Day, and Door. | Index up, other fingers touch thumb | ___________ |
| 5 | E | /EE/ | E is for Eat, Eye, and Energy. | Fingertips curled to thumb, thumb tucked | ___________ |
| 6 | F | /EF/ | F is for Family, Fish, and Flower. | Three fingers up, index and thumb circle | ___________ |
| 7 | G | /JEE/ | G is for Good, Green, and Garden. | Index and thumb point sideways | ___________ |
| 8 | H | /AYCH/ | H is for Happy, Home, and Help. | Index and middle point sideways, stacked | ___________ |
| 9 | I | /EYE/ | I is for I, Ice, and Important. | Pinky finger up, rest closed | ___________ |
| 10 | J | /JAY/ | J is for Juice, Job, and January. | Pinky up, trace letter J in air | ___________ |
| 11 | K | /KAY/ | K is for Kitchen, Key, and Kind. | Index and middle up, thumb between | ___________ |

> **🤟 All Heroes Tip:** *"Fingerspelling is a SUPERPOWER! With 26 handshapes, you can spell ANY English word in ASL. Practice A through M, then keep going!"*

## Scene 3: ASL Numbers 0–10

*Numbers are everywhere — now learn to show them with your hands!*

| # | Word | Phonetic | In the Story... | ASL Sign | My Language |
|---|------|----------|----------------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Zero | /ZEER-oh/ | Zero means nothing or none. | O-hand shape | ___________ |
| 2 | One | /WUN/ | One is a single item. | Index finger up | ___________ |
| 3 | Two | /TOO/ | Two means a pair. | Index and middle up | ___________ |
| 4 | Three | /THREE/ | Three comes after two. | Thumb, index, and middle up | ___________ |
| 5 | Four | /FOR/ | Four fingers but no thumb. | Four fingers up, thumb folded | ___________ |
| 6 | Five | /FYV/ | Five is a whole hand. | All five fingers spread open | ___________ |
| 7 | Six | /SIKS/ | Six is five plus one. | Pinky meets thumb, three fingers up | ___________ |
| 8 | Seven | /SEV-en/ | Seven days in a week. | Ring finger meets thumb, three up | ___________ |
| 9 | Eight | /AYT/ | Eight legs on a spider. | Middle finger meets thumb, three up | ___________ |
| 10 | Nine | /NYN/ | Nine is almost ten. | Index meets thumb, four fingers up | ___________ |
| 11 | Ten | /TEN/ | Ten fingers, ten toes. | A-hand (thumb up), shake | ___________ |

> **🤟 All Heroes Tip:** *"Practice counting 1 to 10 in ASL every morning! Then count BACKWARDS from 10 to 1. Speed it up each day!"*
